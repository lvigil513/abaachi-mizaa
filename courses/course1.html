Course 1 - Greetings and Introductions

Greetings and Introductions

Overview

This course will introduce you to the basics of common greetings and making an introduction in the Jicarilla language.

Vocabulary

  • Hello. — Dáanzho.
  • Good Morning. — Dáanzho nłda. / Nłda gozho.
  • Good Afternoon. — Dáanzho ił ni ji gha i a.
  • How are you? — Ha’dee ánnsi?
  • How is he / she? — Ha’dee asi?
  • How are they? — Ha’dee asi?
  • I am fine. — Doo ánnsi.
  • He / She is fine. — Doo asi.
  • They are fine. — Doo asi.
  • And you? — Dísha?
  • I am fine too. — Shigo a’ doo ánnsi.
  • What is my name? — Ha’dao shizhi?
  • What is your name? — Ha’dao nzhi?
  • What is his / her name? — Ha’dao mizhi?
  • What is their name? — Din ha’dao mizhi?
  • My name is John. — John shizhi.
  • Your name is Mary. — Mary nzhi.
  • His / Her name is Alex. — Alex mizhi.
  • I don’t know what your name is. — Nzhi doo megosi.
  • I don’t know what his / her name is. — Mizhi doo megosi.
  • How old are you? — Da’kwii neeshni?
  • How old is he / she? — Da’kwii me’ee shi?
  • I am 21 years old. — Náadin dáła’éé sheh shi.
  • You are 21 years old. — Náadin dáła’éé neeshni.
  • He / She is 21 years old. — Náadin dáła’éé me’ee shi.
  • I am Jicarilla. — Abáachi ánsht’ii.
  • I am red clan. — Llanero ánsht’ii.
  • I am white clan. — Ollero ánsht’ii.
  • Where are you from? — Ha’shii nanda?
  • I am from Albuquerque. — Gołgeegi shii naashá.
  • I am from Chama. — Chama shii naashá.
  • I am from Dulce. — Lósii shii naashá.
  • I am from Farmington. — Farmington shii naashá.
  • Where do you work? — Ha’yeh (na) na’izii?
  • Do you work at the casino? — Hee ishdika’ee na’izii?
  • Do you work at the clinic? — Hee nzhónách’idle’ee na’izii?
  • Do you work at the school? — Hee Isgwéela’ee na’izii?
  • Do you work at the senior center? — Hee Haskin da ii ya’ee na’izii?
  • Do you work at the store? — Hee denda’ee na’izii? / Hee na’échi’ee na’izii?
  • Do you work at the tribal building? — Hee nant'áan dasida’ee na’izii?
  • I work at the casino. — Ishdika’ee na’isii.
  • I work at the clinic. — Nzhónách’idle’ee na’isii.
  • I work at the school. — Isgwéela’ee na’isii.
  • I work at the senior center. — Haskin da ii ya’ee na’isii.
  • I work at the store. — Denda’ee na’isii. / Na’échi’ee na’isii.
  • I work at the tribal building. — Nant'áan dasida’ee na’isii.

Practice

1. Practice writing your name.

2. An alternative way to write your name.

3. Practice typing your age.

4. Practice writing where you're from.

Review

1. In Jicarilla, how do you say "Hello"?

2. In Jicarilla, how do you say "Good Morning"?

3. In Jicarilla, how do you say "Good Afternoon"?

4. "Nłda gozho" can also mean Good Morning.

5. In Jicarilla, how do you say "How are you?"

6. In Jicarilla, how would you ask someone what their name is?

7. Is this statement true? -> "I am Jicarilla. - Abáachi ánsht’ii."

8. You want to introduce your friend Alex to someone in Jicarilla, how would you begin?

9. In Jicarilla, how would you ask someone where they're from?

10. In Jicarilla, how would you ask your friend where they work?
